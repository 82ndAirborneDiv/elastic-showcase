Is there any danger from receiving extra doses of a vaccine? (42 people like this)

Most of the time, your risk of serious side effects doesn’t increase if you get extra doses of a vaccine. The risk of a reaction at the injection site following certain injected vaccines, such as DTaP (diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis) (dif-THEER-ee-uh, TET-n-uhs, and per-TUHS-is) or pneumococcal vaccine, increases if the doses are not separated by the recommended amount of time.
